## Design Context

### Users
Mixed audience — tech recruiters scanning for qualifications, engineering leads evaluating technical depth, and peers/collaborators exploring shared interests. Visitors arrive with varying technical literacy, so the interface must communicate competence at a glance while rewarding deeper exploration for those who dig in.

### Brand Personality
**Elegant, calm, cerebral.** A thoughtful craftsman who is measured, refined, and intellectually rigorous. The site should feel like a well-organized mind — everything in its place, nothing gratuitous. Confidence expressed through restraint, not volume.

### Emotional Goals
- **Impressed** — "This person clearly knows what they're doing"
- **Intrigued** — "I want to learn more about their work and thinking"
- **Trust** — "This person is reliable and serious about their craft"

### Aesthetic Direction
**Reference:** Linear / Vercel — dark, polished SaaS aesthetic with glass effects, precise spacing, and subtle glow.

**Current implementation aligns well:** near-black backgrounds (#020202), sky-blue primary (#38bdf8), glassmorphism cards with backdrop-blur, mesh gradients, and Material Design 3-inspired token naming. The visual language is already in the right territory.

**Anti-patterns to avoid:**
- Playful or whimsical elements (bouncy animations, emoji, rounded-friendly shapes)
- Cluttered layouts or information overload
- Generic portfolio templates or stock-photo aesthetics
- Overly flashy or gratuitous animation

### Typography
- **Display:** Syne — geometric, distinctive, used for hero headlines
- **Body/Headline/Label:** Plus Jakarta Sans — clean geometric sans-serif, good readability
- Micro-labels use extreme letter-spacing (0.6em), uppercase, 10px — establishing a technical, editorial hierarchy

### Color System
Dark-only, Material Design 3-inspired tokens:
- **Primary:** `#38bdf8` (sky-blue) — used sparingly for accents, CTAs, and interactive elements
- **Tertiary:** `#818cf8` (indigo) — secondary accent
- **Surfaces:** Near-black gradient from `#000000` to `#111111`
- **Text:** `#f8fafc` (on-surface) / `#94a3b8` (on-surface-variant)
- Selection highlight uses primary at 30% opacity

### Motion
Subtle and purposeful — never decorative:
- Custom easing: `[0.22, 1, 0.36, 1]` (snappy deceleration)
- Small transforms: 16px y-offset for fade-up, 0.97 scale for scale-in
- Staggered reveals with tight timing (70ms between children)
- Hover states: gentle lifts (y: -2 to -3), slight scale (1.03), glow shadows

### Spacing & Layout
- Max-width: 7xl (80rem)
- Generous vertical rhythm: 24–48 spacing units between major sections
- Responsive padding: 6 (mobile) to 12 (desktop)
- Cards use large border-radius (2rem) with generous internal padding

### Design Principles

1. **Restraint is confidence.** Every element earns its place. If it doesn't serve comprehension or navigation, remove it. White space is not empty — it's breathing room that signals intentionality.

2. **Precision over decoration.** Prefer tight tracking, exact alignment, and consistent token usage over ornamental flourishes. The design language is engineering-grade: systematic, measurable, reproducible.

3. **Depth through subtlety.** Glass effects, mesh gradients, and micro-interactions create dimensionality without overwhelming. A 1px border at 8% opacity does more than a drop shadow.

4. **Progressive disclosure.** Lead with impact (bold headlines, stat cards), reveal detail on engagement (hover states, scroll reveals). Respect the visitor's time and attention.

5. **Dark-first, light-touch.** The dark palette is the identity, not a mode toggle. Accent color is used like a highlighter — sparingly and with purpose. When in doubt, desaturate.
